Wet Markets in Singapore

Wet markets in Singapore is a data visualisation project that explores the spatial distribution of wet markets and supermarkets in relation to population density. While wet markets have historically played a key role in providing Singaporeans with access to fresh food, they are declining in popularity in recent years. More Singaporeans are choosing to shop at supermarkets.

The storymap visualises open-source data from data.gov.sg to map the distribution of wet markets and supermarkets, layered over population density. This spatial analysis seeks to answer key questions:

  • Are wet markets evenly distributed throughout the neighbourhoods?
  • How accessible are wet markets in an area of high population density?

Despite the declining popularity of wet markets, the government continues to invest in upgrading their infrastructure, as seen in projects like the Bukit Timah Market redevelopment. This raises another question:

  • Is the declining popularity of wet markets due to their infrastructure or are there other reasons?
This question can only be answered through community participation, therefore we have implemented a crowdsourced map that will allow users to report the wet markets they frequent through the Ushahidi platform. This survey seeks to capture public sentiment and understand whether infrastructure plays a role in declining usage, or if other factors—such as convenience or lifestyle change—play a part.
